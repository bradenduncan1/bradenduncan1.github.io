Visual Hierarchy

Spotify

Spotify.com

Spotify shows good use of visual hierarchy as they clearly prioritize where the user's attention is drawn. Especially on the mobile version I am drawn immediately to the good contrast between the background and front text. They finish off the attention grab with a bright green button that tells you to "GET SPOTIFY FREE"

Hick's Law

Church of Jesus Christ

ChurchofJesusChrist.org

The website for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints is a good example for Hick's Law. The website gives major updates in large, visually helpful icons. The most important headlines and events will be noted first giving the user less immediate options when approaching the site.

White Space

Quip

getquip.com

Quip is another example of good UX. Quip takes a large image that simply tells the user what they're offering. It combines space along with meaningful gaps in content. The picture is large on desktop or mobile to give the user a sense of simplicity. White space is effective in giving the user enough of "nothing" to appreciate the beauty of the content more.
